# Where this came from

I was sitting in vocational school (Berufsschule — the part of the German apprenticeship system where you alternate between a company and a classroom) when the person next to me showed me something on their laptop: an HTML file with a Fortnite wallpaper, a proper calculator — start time, contracted hours, break duration, whether you're an apprentice or over 18 — all to compute the exact minute you're allowed to leave.

I understood the feeling. But I also thought: that's the wrong direction entirely. If you're spending real effort figuring out the earliest moment you can leave, you're already somewhere you don't want to be. And that's not always something you can change overnight. But as Goethe wrote in Faust: *selbst ist der Mann* — you have to do it yourself. You still have the day. You still learned something, even if nobody asked you to.

That was the idea. Not a productivity system, not a performance tracker for your employer. Something that faces the other way — that catches what you actually picked up today and holds onto it, because most of it just evaporates otherwise.

I'm 18. This is my first project I've shipped. There are gaps in it and there will probably be a rebuild at some point. But "finish it when it's perfect" is how projects die, and I'd rather have something real and incomplete than something ideal that doesn't exist.
